Marie Curie

1867 - 1934 | Pioneer in Radioactivity Research

The First Woman to Win a Nobel Prize

Marie Curie was a Polish-born physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, the first person and only woman to win the Nobel Prize twice, and the only person to win the Nobel Prize in two different scientific fields.

Scientific Contributions

  • Pioneered research on radioactivity (a term she coined)
  • Developed techniques for isolating radioactive isotopes
  • Discovered two elements: polonium and radium
  • Founded the Curie Institutes in Paris and Warsaw
  • Her work laid the foundation for modern radiation therapy in medicine

Legacy

Marie Curie's groundbreaking work changed science forever. Despite facing discrimination as a woman in science, she persisted in her research. Her notebooks are still so radioactive they're kept in lead-lined boxes. The curie (a unit of radioactivity) is named in honor of her and Pierre's work.
